# META ADS PLAYBOOK — On-Screen Text, Hooks, CTAs & Placement Rules

> Drop a copy of this file into each team's Guidelines folder
> (`<asset_root>/<team>/Guidelines/` or `Brand Guidelines/`) so Kino reads it
> alongside the brand book on every automated run. These rules apply to EVERY
> video produced for Meta placements (Facebook/Instagram Feed, Reels, Stories).

---

## 1. THE FRAME — safe zones (9:16 · 1080×1920)

Meta's UI covers parts of every vertical ad. Anything placed in a covered zone
is invisible or unclickable to the viewer.

| Zone | Pixels (1080×1920) | Covered by |
|---|---|---|
| TOP 14% | y = 0–270 | profile bar, username, timestamp |
| BOTTOM 35% | y = 1250–1920 | caption text, CTA button, handle, audio ticker |
| RIGHT ~11% | x = 960–1080 | like / comment / share / menu rail |
| LEFT margin | keep ≥ 60px clear | breathing room, ultra-tall crops |

**Usable canvas: roughly x 60–950, y 270–1250.** For other aspect ratios,
scale the percentages (14% top, 35% bottom) — never the raw pixel numbers.

**Center-square strategy:** the most critical content (hook, offer, product)
should ALSO survive a crop to 1:1 or 4:5 for Feed placement. Keep it inside a
centered ~950×950 square when possible.

**Hard rule — ALL TEXT FULLY IN FRAME.** No overlay may touch the frame edge
or bleed past it. Wrap long lines; shrink the font before you ever let a word
clip. If a line can't fit at readable size, shorten the copy instead.

---

## 2. LAYOUT — where each element lives

Three text layers, top to bottom. They must never overlap each other.

1. **VISUAL HOOK** — top of frame, first ~3 seconds
   - Top edge at **y ≈ 300px** (just below the top UI zone).
   - 5–8 words max. One idea. If it wraps past 3 lines, cut words.
   - White bold text on a ~65%-opacity black box (or brand-styled equivalent).
2. **CAPTIONS** — dead center of frame (true center, centered horizontally)
   - Short chunks (≤ ~20 chars/line), word-synced to the VO.
   - Center placement is NON-NEGOTIABLE for Meta deliverables.
3. **ON-SCREEN CTA** — ~200px below the captions
   - Must stay ABOVE the bottom-35% UI zone even when it wraps.
   - Font must be a different family than the captions (contrast = attention).
   - Visible either for the ENTIRE video or only the FINAL 30% — nothing else.
   - Copy is an OFFER from the brand guidelines (see §4).

---

## 3. HOOKS — copy patterns that stop the scroll (2026)

The hook has ~1.3 seconds to earn a pause. Write it as on-screen text AND make
the spoken open reinforce it. Proven UGC patterns:

- **Call-out:** "Homeowners over 40, listen up" / "If your car is out of warranty…"
- **Negative/contrarian:** "Stop overpaying for car repairs" / "You're using your warranty wrong"
- **Cost anchor:** "This repair bill was $4,200" / "I almost paid $900 for this"
- **POV / relatable:** "POV: your check-engine light just came on"
- **Curiosity gap:** "Nobody tells you this about extended warranties"
- **Number promise:** "3 things to check before your warranty expires"
- **Time pressure:** "Before you pay another repair bill, watch this"

Rules of thumb:
- 5–8 words. Sentence case or ALL CAPS — pick one per video, don't mix.
- No brand name in the hook (the brand pays off later; the hook earns the pause).
- Never a question that can be answered "no" and scrolled past.
- The first b-roll shot under the hook should visually match the hook's subject.

---

## 4. CTAs — always drive the ad click, never anywhere else

Meta ads carry their own link/button. Every CTA — spoken AND on-screen — points
the viewer at the ad's own click-through. **Never** a website, URL, or phone
number, even if the brand book lists one; translate it.

**Spoken CTA (last 5–8s of VO):**
- "Tap the link below to get your free quote."
- "Click below — it takes two minutes."
- "I've left the link right down below."
- "Hit the button below to see if you qualify."

**On-screen CTA (text overlay):**
- Lead with the OFFER, stated in the brand guidelines: free quote, $ discount,
  limited promo, free consultation — whatever the brand book actually offers.
- 4–8 words, action-first: "GET YOUR FREE QUOTE TODAY", "$300 OFF — CLAIM BELOW",
  "FREE QUOTE · TAP BELOW".
- Banned in any CTA: "visit www…", "call 1-800…", "go to our website",
  QR codes, app-store badges.

Timing: on-screen CTA runs full-length OR last 30% of the video. Spoken CTA
always closes the script.

---

## 5. CAPTIONS — style rules

- Word-synced chunks; 1 line preferred (2 max), ≤ ~20 characters per line.
- Min on-screen duration ~1.5s per chunk; no flicker cuts.
- High-contrast box style (brand colors); consistent across the whole video.
- Never restyle mid-video; never cover the CTA or the hook.
- Keep punctuation minimal — captions are rhythm, not prose.

---

## 6. PACING & B-ROLL

- Cut every **1.8–5 seconds**. Static shots die on Reels.
- Vary footage across variants: different clips, different order, and different
  in-points within the same clip (a 6s clip can supply 1–4s in one video and
  3–6s in the next).
- First shot must match the hook's subject. Last shot should sit calm under the
  CTA (no busy motion fighting the offer text).
- Action-only b-roll under VO — no talking-head footage under a voiceover
  (lip-sync mismatch reads as broken).

---

## 7. SCRIPT STRUCTURE (30–40s target)

| Beat | Length | Job |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 2–4s | mirror the on-screen hook, earn the pause |
| Problem | 6–10s | the pain, concrete and specific ($ figures work) |
| Solution | 10–15s | the brand's answer, benefits over features |
| Proof / trust | 4–6s | social proof, guarantee, plan coverage |
| CTA | 5–8s | offer + "tap the link below" |

- ~2.5 words/second at natural ElevenLabs pace (≈ 88 words for 35s).
- Short punchy sentences. No clause stacking. Read it aloud — if you'd trip, cut it.
- Every claim must be permitted by the brand guidelines. No superlatives
  without evidence, no medical/legal claims, no competitor mentions.

---

## 8. COMPLIANCE QUICK-CHECK (before any render ships)

- [ ] All text fully in frame, nothing in the top-14% / bottom-35% / right-rail zones
- [ ] Captions dead-center, consistent style
- [ ] Hook ≤ 8 words, first 3s, top placement
- [ ] On-screen CTA = brand-book offer, different font than captions, full or last-30% timing
- [ ] No URLs / phone numbers anywhere (spoken or on-screen)
- [ ] Cuts every 1.8–5s, no talking heads under VO
- [ ] Script beats: hook → problem → solution → proof → CTA
